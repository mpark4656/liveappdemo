Alan Mathison Turing, OBE FRS

The Father of Theoretical Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence

Turing played a crucial role in cracking intercepted coded messages that enabled the Allies to defeat the Nazis in many crucial engagements.

Here is a timeline of Dr. Turing's life.

  • 1912: Born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1926: Went to Sherborne School
  • 1931: Studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge
  • 1938: Worked part-time with the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS)
  • 1939: Reported to Bletchley Park
  • 1940: Developed the Bombe to decipher the messages sent by the Germans using their Enigma machine
  • 1945: Awarded the OBE for his wartime services
  • 1951: Elected Fellow of the Royal Society
  • 1954: Died in his home in Wilmslow, Cheshire, from poisoning
"Those who can imagine anything, can create the impossible" - Alan Turing
